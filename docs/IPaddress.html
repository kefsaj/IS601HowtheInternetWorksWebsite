IP Addresses, Packets and Routing



IP address is string of numbers specified as the internet protocol address assigned to each device. It is four numbers separated by dots.

Packet is a fixed size unit of data. It is used on to transport information accross the internet.

Routing is the choosing the most viable path to sent data the network.

Continuing the example of the house, the IP address is the street address that is assigned to your property.

Packets can be like thought of like transporting a large object from one place to another. It is much easier and simpler to split and divide up the object than to move it as a whole. An example would be buying furniture, the pieces are divided and packeted in a way that it is easily transportable and easily assembled at the destination, just like information is divided into packets and reformed to be a whole at the end to form what the result would be.

Routing can be thought of as how a gps might guide you to your destination. Choosing the the best way to your destination.

The IP address keeps the internet organized. Every device connected to the internet is assigned a IP address, yet there not been any conflicts for lack of IP address due to NAT. With NAT, the network router acts as the middle man routing where each packet goes to. In the network with having huge amounts of IP address that would cause conflicts due to the lack of IP address availability in correlation to the number of people in the world.

Packets will break up information into smaller chunks of data to be easily sent across the net.

There are two types, static and dynamic. In static routing, a path is manually hence a change in routing will not be corrected automatically.In Dynamic routing, routes are calculated and denied by software in regarding to network situation.
